# AIRCA Notes v1

## Working premise

As AI makes answers cheaper, the scarce resource becomes decision capacity.

This note explores AIRCA as an AI-era adaptation of RACI, especially in environments where AI can surface signals, generate options, rank possibilities, and support execution.

## Why AIRCA may matter

Traditional RACI clarified responsibility in workflow-driven environments.

AIRCA may become more useful where the central challenge is no longer only coordination of work, but coordination of decision-making under conditions of AI-assisted overload.

## AIRCA

- **Architect**: define goals, guardrails, thresholds, and criteria
- **Inform**: surface relevant signals, context, and candidate options
- **Rank**: filter and prioritize realistic decision paths
- **Commit**: attach human ownership and accountability
- **Act**: execute through workflow, automation, or operations

## Extension into fractal structure

The extension developed in this repository is that AIRCA may scale better if supported by:

- fractal taxonomy
- fractal ontology
- fractal decision architecture

The same governing logic should recur across levels:

- enterprise
- team
- workflow
- document
- issue
- task

## Open question

If AI increases the volume of possible answers, recommendations, and actions, what framework best preserves human agency and accountability?

AIRCA may be one answer, especially if paired with recursive knowledge structures and machine-readable governance artifacts.
