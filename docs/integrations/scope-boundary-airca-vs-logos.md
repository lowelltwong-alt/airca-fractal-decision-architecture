# Scope Boundary: AIRCA vs. Logos

## Core boundary

This repository is intended to remain primarily a **secular business and governance architecture** repository.

Its core purpose is to develop AIRCA, fractal ontology, fractal decision-making, machine-readable governance artifacts, and related decision architecture for institutional and business use.

## What belongs here

This repository should prioritize:

- secular business decision architecture
- governance and accountability design
- organizational ontology and taxonomy
- machine-readable artifacts for workflow and decision systems
- AIRCA as a cross-domain decision spine
- policy, operational, legal, financial, and strategic use cases

## What does not belong here as a primary buildout

This repository should **not** become a theology-heavy doctrinal repository.

Christian doctrinal adaptation, Logos-grounded theological architecture, and deeper doctrinal system buildout should primarily live in the dedicated Logos project.

## Relationship to the Logos project

The Logos project is the right place for:

- Christian doctrinal clusters
- theological orthodoxy mapping
- tradition-specific doctrinal assessments
- scripture interpretation layers tied to doctrine
- Logos-grounded ultimate-frame development

AIRCA may still provide reusable structural patterns that Logos can adapt, but the center of gravity for Christian doctrinal expansion belongs there rather than here.

## Practical rule going forward

For this repository:

- keep examples predominantly secular and business-facing
- keep ontology and scoring logic domain-neutral where possible
- treat theological material only as a light comparative or transfer example when necessary
- direct doctrine-heavy implementation work to the Logos repository

## Design consequence

This means AIRCA remains:

- broadly reusable
- institutionally legible
- business-oriented
- governance-oriented
- adaptable across domains without being defined by a theological implementation

And Logos remains:

- the Christian doctrinal and theological adaptation layer
- the place for deeper doctrinal graph buildout
- the place where explicit theological truth-claims are modeled in depth
